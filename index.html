CookMate Vision

CookMate is a sleek and simple tool for people looking to elevate their cooking to the next level, providing unique recipe ideas based on what‚Äôs in the kitchen.

The app solves common cooking challenges like meal planning, recipe block, time management, and dietary needs. It offers a wide variety of recipes organized by categories such as prep time and dietary restrictions, making it easy for users to find meals that match their preferences. Upon opening the app, users are greeted by a curated recipe list to jumpstart their creativity. The built-in cook-timer simplifies tracking cooking times and ensures accuracy.

With easy access to a diverse list of recipes, CookMate inspires creativity, supports users in reaching dietary goals, and helps them stay engaged in their cooking journey. Users can search for specific recipes, and even contribute their own with personal notes and images.

The favourites tab allows users to quickly access preferred dishes, while each recipe detail page includes an image, ingredients, and a vivid description. CookMate is ideal for those who want to diversify their meals, learn new recipes, and enjoy a smoother cooking experience.

Success Metrics: 1,000 user reviews in the first year with an average rating of 4.5/5 and 80% user retention over one year.

Intended Users

CookMate is designed for:

  • Home cooks of all experience levels
  • Busy individuals needing quick meal ideas
  • People with dietary restrictions (vegetarian, gluten-free, etc.)
  • Users who enjoy personalizing their own recipes
  • Anyone looking to reduce food waste by cooking with available ingredients

CookMate Features Overview

  • Timer
    A built-in cooking timer designed to help users track recipe steps accurately.
  • Notes
    Allows users to add personal notes while cooking, making it easier to remember tips and adjustments.
  • Recipe Detail
    A detailed view of each recipe including ingredients, steps, and images.
  • Cooking Instruction
    Step-by-step cooking instructions with interactive timers and note support.
  • Favourites

  • Add Recipe
    Users can contribute their own recipes, complete with images, instructions, and personal notes.
  • Search
    Quickly find specific recipes using keywords or filters like dietary preference and cook time.
  • Explore Recipes
    Browse recommended and trending recipes to get inspired based on what's in your kitchen.

Development Postmortem

‚úÖ What Went Well

One of the major improvements in Iteration 2 was task delegation. Each team member was assigned a single feature at the beginning, which resulted in more organized work and a balanced distribution of responsibilities. Additionally, features like the recipe detail view, filtering system, cooking instructions, and explore page were implemented effectively and integrated without major conflicts.

We also started applying design patterns like Singleton, Factory Method, Adapter, and Observer in key parts of our application. These decisions helped simplify our architecture and improve maintainability.

Despite recognizing the need for more consistent meetings in our Iteration 1 retrospective, we failed to significantly improve this area in Iteration 2. Communication delays persisted, with questions often going unanswered for long periods. Scheduling regular team check-ins is something we need to prioritize going forward.

We also encountered technical debt due to rushed development ‚Äî for example, improper placement of code in the main method in `CookingInstructionActivity`, and a misused static inner class in `RecipeAdapter`. These issues were identified and refactored post-implementation.

üîÑ What We‚Äôd Do Differently

If we had the chance to restart, we would enforce a stricter communication schedule, start using a shared task board earlier (such as GitHub issues with milestones), and plan time for code review to avoid technical debt buildup.

üí° Lessons Learned

We learned the importance of readable, maintainable code ‚Äî several of us created utility methods and helper classes to improve clarity and reusability. Agile practices such as branching by feature, retrospectives, and user stories helped us stay focused even when things got busy.
